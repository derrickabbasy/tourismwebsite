Market

Market

Every week, from Thursday to Sunday, dabble and sample the very best of Liverpool's independent foodie vendors will be giving you a delicious taste of the city's ever-growing indie food scene. It is set in one of the warehouses of the old Cains Brewery in the Baltic Triangle, it's the perfect addition to the up-and-coming trendy part of town that everyone wants a piece of when they come to visit. Get your hands on everything from halloumi dogs and waffle fries from Meet Frank to the melt-in-the-mouth modern small plates from Oktopus (we recommend the popcorn mussels - delicious). Head to the bar for a variety of drink options to wash it all down too. With more traders expected to fill the market in the future, don't leave it too long until your next trip. If you're heading here on a Sunday, perfect - the Baltic Market regularly hosts arts & crafts and farmer's markets, so every weekend is different from the last.

History & Heritage

History & Heritage

It is situated opposite Lime Street station, where the St George’s Hall provides a warm and beautiful welcome to Liverpool, reflecting the pride and passion of the Victorians who built this land-mark venue. The hall is also one of the finest examples of a neoclassical building in the world and it stands majestically over the city skyline. It is opened in 1854 and the outstanding expression of the confidence and ambition the Liverpool people had for their city, their delight in profiling their achievements is particularly personified by the remarkble design of the Concert Room and the grandeur of The Great Hall. Those who designed, built and funded for this extraordinary building, sought deliberately to lift the human heart.
