Rufford Old Hall

Rufford Old Hall is located at a 34-minute drive from Liverpool. This Tudor building was the home of the Hesketh family and has 500 years of history. Its houses the Edwardian gardens, Squirrel Border, the South Lawn, Orchard Paddock, the North Paddock, and you’ll have the chance to see the beautiful blooming nepeta, roses, wisteria and the wildflowers along the border.

Rules in the Liverpool :

You must be able to read a vehicle number plate, in daylight, from a distance of 20 metres – or 20.5 metres where there’s an old style number plate. And if you need to wear glasses (or contact lenses) to do it, you must wear them at all times while driving.

You must obey signs used by school crossing patrols.

You must obey all traffic light signals and traffic signs giving orders – and that includes temporary signals & signs.

You’re only allowed to flash your headlights to let other road users know that you are there. You could be prosecuted for using them to convey any other message – or to intimidate other road users.

Car horns can be used only while your vehicle is moving and you need to warn other road users of your presence. Sounding your horn aggressively can be an offence, and the Highway Code says you must not use it while stationary on the road or when driving in a built-up area between the hours of 11.30pm and 7am – except when another road user poses a danger.

All sidelights and rear registration plate lights must be lit between sunset and sunrise.

You must not use any lights in a way which would dazzle or cause discomfort to other road users, including pedestrians, cyclists and horse riders

It’s against the code to leave a parked vehicle unattended with the engine running or leave a vehicle engine running unnecessarily while the vehicle is stationary on a public road.

An obvious one, and one that annoys most of us being overtaken by speeding drivers – you must not exceed the maximum speed limits for the road and for your vehicle. The presence of street lights generally means that there is a 30 mph speed limit, unless otherwise specified

That one-way street might look like a tempting short cut, but the Code says you must always travel in the direction indicated by signs.

You must not drive on or over a pavement, footpath or bridleway except to gain lawful access to property – or in the case of an emergency.

You must not throw anything out of a vehicle, including food, paper or cigarette ends.

Quarantine & Customs

Domestic Quarantine. When travelling in regional Liverpool, please heed local advice about carrying food, as quarantine regulations restrict travellers from carrying plants and fruit into some regional areas. Affected areas are signposted, have roadside quarantine fruit disposal bins in place, and may stage quarantine roadblocks.

Fruit Fly. Fruit flies lay eggs in fruit and vegetables, rendering produce inedible and devastating crops. Fruit fly can be spread by travellers carrying infested fruit into or between horticultural regions of Liverpool. Please help protect Victoria's horticulture by heeding all local advice about travelling with fruit, and by disposing of fruit in quarantine bins as necessary.

UK Custom Regulations.You can bring some goods from abroad without having to pay UK tax or ‘duty’ (customs charges), as long as they’re for your own use. It depends on the type of goods you're bringing in and where you're travelling from. If you are travelling to the UK from the EU, you can bring an unlimited amount of most goods (such as cigarettes and alcohol) for your own use without paying tax or duty. This legislation does not apply to some new member states. Check the full duty-free regulations. If you are travelling to the UK from outside the EU, you're allowed to bring a certain amount of goods such as alcohol, tobacco, perfume, souvenirs for your own use or as gifts. Your personal allowance includes up to 1 litre of spirits, 200 cigarettes and up to £390-worth of other goods. Find out more about UK customs regulations on the official UK government website.
